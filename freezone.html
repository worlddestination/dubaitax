📋 QFZP CRITERIA

The 5 Conditions for 0% Tax

All five must be met continuously to maintain Qualifying Free Zone Person status.

01
Maintained in a UAE Free Zone
▶
The company must be incorporated, registered, or established in a UAE Free Zone and carry out its core activities from within the free zone. Simply having a registered office is not sufficient — the business must have genuine operational substance in the zone.

✅ Required: FZ Trade License & physical presence
02
95% Qualifying Income Threshold
▶
At least 95% of the entity's total revenue must be derived from Qualifying Activities or Qualifying Transactions. Non-qualifying income (such as mainland UAE sales to consumers) must remain below 5% of total revenue.

If this threshold is breached in any tax period, the entity loses QFZP status for that entire year and the 9% rate applies to all profits.

⚠️ Monitor monthly to avoid accidental breach
03
Adequate Economic Substance
▶
The entity must demonstrate real economic substance in the UAE, which means:

• Adequate number of qualified employees working in the FZ
• Sufficient operating expenditure incurred in the UAE
• Adequate physical assets present in the FZ
• Core income generating activities conducted in the UAE

📋 FTA may request substance evidence during audits
04
Audited Separate Financial Statements
▶
The QFZP must prepare and maintain separate audited financial statements specifically for the free zone entity. These statements must be prepared in accordance with IFRS or UAE GAAP by an FTA-approved auditor.

Financial statements must be available on request and must clearly separate FZ income from any mainland income.

📄 Annual audit deadline: 9 months after financial year end
05
Arm's Length Transactions & Transfer Pricing
▶
All transactions with related parties and connected persons must be conducted on arm's length terms — at prices that would apply between independent parties in comparable transactions.

Entities that are part of multinational groups or have significant related-party dealings must also maintain transfer pricing documentation as per FTA guidelines (Master File and Local File requirements).

⚖️ Non-arm's length transactions can trigger 9% rate

❓ FREQUENTLY ASKED

Free Zone Tax FAQs

▶Can a QFZP sell to UAE mainland customers?
Yes, but only up to 5% of total annual revenue. Sales beyond this threshold will cause the entity to lose its QFZP status for the entire tax year, making all profits subject to 9% corporate tax.
▶What happens if I lose QFZP status?
If QFZP status is lost, the 9% corporate tax rate applies to all taxable income for that tax year. QFZP status can be reapplied in subsequent years once eligibility criteria are re-met.
▶Is a QFZP exempt from filing corporate tax returns?
No. QFZPs must still register for corporate tax, file annual returns, and maintain records. The 0% rate is a tax exemption on qualifying income, not an exemption from the filing obligation.
▶Does QFZP status apply to VAT as well?
No. QFZP is a corporate tax concept only. VAT obligations are entirely separate. If your FZ business makes taxable supplies exceeding AED 375,000 annually, VAT registration is required regardless of QFZP status.
▶Can holding companies in free zones qualify?
Yes — holding companies that hold shares in UAE or foreign subsidiaries and earn dividend/capital gain income can qualify as QFZP, provided they meet the substance and income-source requirements.
▶What is the Small Business Relief threshold for 2026?
The SBR threshold for UAE taxable persons is AED 3,000,000 in annual revenue per tax period. If revenue exceeds this, standard corporate tax (9%) or QFZP rules apply for FZ entities.
▶Are crypto and digital asset businesses QFZP eligible?
Potentially, if operating from a VARA-licensed FZ entity (e.g. DMCC crypto centre) and income qualifies. Trading activities are generally qualifying; advisory services may be subject to further FTA guidance.
▶Do I need a separate FZ bank account?
While not legally mandated by the FTA, maintaining separate bank accounts for FZ operations is strongly recommended for audit purposes and to clearly separate qualifying from non-qualifying income streams.
